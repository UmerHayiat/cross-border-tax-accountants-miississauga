What is HST and How Does it Work?

Definition of HST

Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) is a consumption tax in Ontario that combines both the federal Goods and Services Tax (GST) and the provincial sales tax (PST) into one unified rate. Unlike GST, which is levied only by the federal government, HST is applied at both federal and provincial levels, and it’s the tax that businesses are required to collect on most goods and services.

How HST Works

HST works by businesses charging the tax on goods and services they provide to their customers. The business collects this tax and then remits it to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). Importantly, businesses are also allowed to reclaim the HST they pay on their own business expenses—this process is known as Input Tax Credits (ITCs).

HST Rates in Ontario

In Ontario, the standard HST rate is 13%. This includes 5% for the federal GST and 8% for the provincial portion. However, some goods and services might be subject to reduced rates or even exemptions. For example, most basic groceries are zero-rated, which means no HST is applied to them, even though they are technically a taxable supply.

Do You Need to Register for HST?

Threshold for Registration

If your business earns more than $30,000 in revenue in a single calendar quarter or in four consecutive quarters, you are legally required to register for HST with the CRA. This threshold is set to help smaller businesses avoid the burden of tax collection if they are not yet generating significant income.

Voluntary Registration

Even if your revenue is under the $30,000 threshold, you may still choose to register voluntarily for HST. Doing so can allow you to claim Input Tax Credits (ITCs) for the HST you pay on business expenses, helping reduce overall tax costs.

Who Should Register?

Any business that makes taxable sales, leases, or services in Ontario needs to consider HST registration. This includes retailers, wholesalers, service providers, and even contractors. Whether you're operating from a brick-and-mortar store or an online platform, registration might be mandatory.

How to Register for HST in Mississauga

Steps to Register

  • Gather Your Business Information: Ensure you have your business number (BN), legal name, and address ready.
  • Online Registration: The most convenient way to register for HST is through the CRA website. You can also complete the registration over the phone or in person at a CRA office.
  • Choose Your Filing Frequency: When registering, you will be asked to choose how often you plan to file your HST returns (monthly, quarterly, or annually).

Documents Required

  • Your business number (BN)
  • Your business’ legal name and physical address
  • Estimated revenue for the next 12 months
  • Nature of your business and types of taxable goods and services offered

When to Register

Once your business exceeds the $30,000 threshold, you need to register immediately. If you’re starting a new business and expect your sales to exceed this threshold, it's best to register right away.

Collecting and Remitting HST

Charging HST

As a registered business, you must charge HST on taxable sales and services. The rate is typically 13% in Ontario, but there are exceptions. For example, certain medical services and educational programs are exempt from HST.

Remitting HST

Once collected, you need to remit the HST to the CRA. The frequency at which you file your HST return (monthly, quarterly, or annually) depends on your total revenue. Make sure to file by the appropriate deadlines to avoid penalties.

Input Tax Credits (ITCs)

If your business incurs HST on purchases and expenses, you may be able to reclaim this amount by filing ITCs. This helps lower the overall amount of tax you need to remit.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Common mistakes include failing to register for HST on time, charging the incorrect HST rate, or not claiming ITCs on eligible purchases. Be diligent about keeping accurate records of your sales and expenses to avoid these errors.

HST and Sales Tax on Different Types of Goods and Services

Taxable vs. Exempt Goods and Services

HST is charged on most goods and services in Ontario, but certain items are exempt from the tax. Understanding which products fall under taxable or exempt categories is essential for compliance.

  • Taxable goods and services: Most products, like electronics, clothing, and professional services (consulting, design, etc.).
  • Exempt goods and services: Items such as health care, education services, and financial services (e.g., life insurance premiums) are generally exempt from HST.

Zero-Rated Supplies

Some goods, like basic groceries or certain agricultural products, are zero-rated. This means they are technically subject to tax but at a rate of 0%, so no HST is charged. These items should still be recorded in your HST filings to maintain compliance.

Filing HST Returns in Mississauga

Filing Frequency

The CRA assigns your filing frequency based on your revenue. Most small businesses with revenue under $1.5 million can file annually, while those with higher revenue may be required to file quarterly or monthly.

What You Need to File

  • Total sales
  • Taxable sales
  • ITCs for business expenses

Common Filing Mistakes

Errors in your filing can lead to penalties. Common mistakes include missing the deadline, not claiming all eligible ITCs, or underreporting taxable sales. Double-check your return before submission to ensure accuracy.
